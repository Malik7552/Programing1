About Giraffes

Giraffes are the world's tallest animals, found in Africa, distinguished by their long necks, legs, and spotted coats, with a unique pattern for each individual. These herbivores use their long tongues to eat leaves from tall trees, can reach speeds over 30 mph, and have large, fur-covered horns called ossicones. Giraffes live in social herds called towers, can sleep standing up, and are vital to their ecosystems, though they face threats from habitat loss and poaching.
